National Basketball Association

The National Basketball Association (NBA) is the premier professional basketball league in North America, featuring 30 teams across the United States and Canada. Founded in 1946, the NBA has grown into a global phenomenon, showcasing the world's most talented basketball players and generating billions in revenue through television rights, merchandise, and live events.

The league has produced legendary players like Michael Jordan, LeBron James, and Kobe Bryant, who have become cultural icons beyond the sport. With its fast-paced action, high-flying dunks, and intense competition, the NBA has captured the imagination of fans worldwide, making basketball one of the most popular sports on the planet.

The NBA's commitment to social justice, community engagement, and global expansion has helped it maintain its position as a leader in professional sports. The league has been at the forefront of social change, using its platform to address important issues and promote positive change in society.

Today, the NBA continues to innovate both on and off the court, with advanced analytics, player development programs, and cutting-edge technology enhancing the game. The league's global reach extends to over 200 countries, making it one of the most internationally recognized sports organizations in the world.

League Structure

The NBA is divided into two conferences: the Eastern Conference and the Western Conference, each containing 15 teams. The regular season runs from October to April, followed by the playoffs where 16 teams compete for the NBA Championship. The league's structure promotes intense rivalries and competitive balance, ensuring exciting basketball throughout the season.
